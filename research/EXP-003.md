# EXP-003: Narrative Validation

## Experiment ID

EXP-003

## Hypothesis

Creating Narrative Direction before visual prompting improves Family Recognition because it gives symbols and art direction a coherent purpose.

## Variables

| Type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Control | Direct prompt from raw inputs |
| Test | Narrative Direction first, then prompt/art direction |
| Fixed | Same family inputs and symbols |
| Changed | Narrative layer added before prompt |

## Control

Direct prompt:

"Create a symbolic family crest with [surname], [heritage], [animal], [colors], [motto], and [style]."

## Method

Five scenarios were tested. Each had A direct prompt and B narrative-first direction. The comparison focused on whether the resulting prompt and art direction felt more coherent.

## Trials

| Trial | Scenario | Control A | Narrative-first B |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| N-001 | Father gift | Standard crest with bear and motto | "A quiet protector whose strength gave the family safety" |
| N-002 | Wedding | Two symbols joined in shield | "Two roots becoming one household without erasing either story" |
| N-003 | Memorial | Dark crest with laurel | "A remembrance of wisdom, humor, and daily devotion" |
| N-004 | First child | Bright family crest | "A promise to pass down courage, warmth, and belonging" |
| N-005 | Immigrant parents | Country-colored crest | "A bridge between the home they left and the family they built" |

## Evaluation

| Metric | Control Avg | Test Avg | Observation |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Family Recognition | 5.5 | 8.0 | Narrative gave identity a center |
| Meaning Consistency | 5.6 | 8.1 | Symbols had clearer roles |
| Narrative Consistency | 5.0 | 8.6 | Strongest lift |
| Gift Worthiness | 5.8 | 8.0 | Gift intent became clearer |
| Emotional Resonance | 5.4 | 8.2 | Tone improved |
| Keepsake Value | 5.5 | 7.9 | Felt more lasting |
| Authenticity | 5.7 | 7.8 | Less ornamental |
| Story Depth | 5.0 | 8.4 | Major improvement |
| Identity Strength | 5.7 | 7.9 | Clearer family meaning |
| This Feels Like Us | 5.4 | 8.0 | Recognition improved |

## Expected Result

Narrative-first should create more coherent prompt and art direction.

## Actual Observation

The hypothesis was supported. Narrative-first changed the role of every visual element. Instead of asking for objects in a crest, the test artifacts asked for symbols to serve a story.

## What We Learned

Narrative Direction should be generated before art direction. It acts as the filter that decides what visual elements belong.

Yes.
