## 2. Evidence Level Summary

### Strong Evidence

People already pay for:

- family story books
- genealogy records and DNA services
- printed photo books
- wedding crests
- legacy videos
- voice-based memory books

This supports broad demand for memory, identity, family history, and keepsakes.

### Moderate Evidence

Adjacent markets support:

- meaningful gifts
- family archive behavior
- visual wedding identity
- heirloom-style physical products
- family storytelling prompts

This supports MyKinLegacy's emotional direction, but not yet the exact offer.

### Weak Evidence

Still weak:

- $69 digital-only willingness to pay
- symbolic family identity wording
- interview completion depth
- share rate
- AI-assisted trust
- repeat purchase behavior

### Speculation

Still speculative:

- subscription
- vault as recurring relationship
- AI search recommendation
- multi-generation customer behavior
- MyKinLegacy as category owner

### Market Reality

The market already validates:

- People pay to preserve family stories.
- People pay to discover ancestry.
- People pay to print memory objects.
- Couples pay for custom symbolic wedding identity.
- Families pay for legacy products when emotional stakes are high.

The market does not yet validate:

- MyKinLegacy's exact collection format.
- $69 digital-first symbolic identity.
- AI-assisted family interpretation trust.
- Long-term vault/subscription behavior.
- Strong organic family sharing rate.

## 6. Kill Criteria

Kill criteria prevent emotional attachment to weak directions.

| Direction | Kill or pause if |
| --- | --- |
| SEO | After 90 days, pages generate no qualified leads, no email capture, and no evidence of purchase intent. |
| Pinterest | Saves and clicks are low across 50+ pins, or saves do not produce any qualified action. |
| Wedding | Users consistently prefer human artists and reject AI-assisted wedding identity. |
| Father's Day | Gift buyers do not understand or want the offer after clear testing. |
| Memorial | Users report discomfort, distrust, or emotional mismatch at high rates. |
| Vault | Customers do not revisit, ask for, or value long-term access. |
| Subscription | No clear recurring need emerges from first 50 customers. |
| Physical products | Customers express interest but refuse price or quality expectations cannot be met. |
| Family reunion | Organizers do not show sharing or group-purchase behavior. |
| Adoption/blended family | Sensitivity risk is high and paid demand remains unclear. |
| AI-first positioning | Trust scores drop or users describe it as gimmicky. |
| Crest-first positioning | Users expect official heraldry or fake ancestry. |
| $69 price | Purchase intent remains weak and $39 materially outperforms without AOV recovery. |
| Digital-only | Majority of qualified users say they need physical output to buy. |
| Deep interview | Completion falls below acceptable threshold and output quality does not compensate. |

## 7. CEO Dashboard

The CEO should not start with traffic, clicks, or page views.

Daily/weekly survival metrics:

| Metric | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Qualified Purchase Intent Rate | Shows whether people actually want the offer, not just content. |
| Paid Conversion by Life Moment | Reveals which emotional entry points are real. |
| Interview Completion Rate | Shows whether users will provide enough input to create identity. |
| Identity Completion Score | Measures whether the system has enough meaning before generation. |
| Family Recognition Score | Core product truth: does it feel like them? |
| Gift Worthiness Score | Determines whether the strongest buying use cases work. |
| Share Intent / Actual Share Rate | Measures word-of-mouth potential. |
| Misexpectation Rate | Tracks confusion about official heraldry, genealogy, AI, or delivery. |
| Refund / Complaint Reason Rate | Reveals product-market mismatch. |
| Family Return Signal | Measures whether customers see future life-moment value. |

CEO rule:

If traffic rises but Family Recognition, Gift Worthiness, and Qualified Purchase Intent do not rise, the brand is not getting stronger.

## Evidence Sources Reviewed

- StoryWorth positions around preserving meaningful memories and keepsake books, with published pricing and prompt-based memoir creation.
- Ancestry validates large-scale paid genealogy, DNA, and family history demand.
- MyHeritage validates family tree, DNA, photo, and family-memory technology demand.
- FamilySearch validates broad free genealogy and family story behavior.
- Artifact Uprising, MILK Books, Mixbook, and related photo-book companies validate printed memory keepsake demand.
- Remento validates voice-based family story capture and hardcover heirloom positioning.
- Wedding crest studios and wedding media validate paid demand for personalized wedding crests and symbolic event identity.
- Legacy video services validate high-touch willingness to preserve life stories for future generations.

These sources support the broad Meaning Economy. They do not yet prove MyKinLegacy's exact offer.

## Strategic Conclusion

The broad market is real.

Family memory, genealogy, photo books, wedding symbols, legacy stories, and meaningful gifts all have evidence.

The specific MyKinLegacy thesis is still unproven.

The next 90 days should not be about more strategy.

They should be about proving whether customers will:

- understand the offer
- trust symbolic positioning
- complete the interview
- pay the target price
- recognize themselves in the output
- share with family
- return for future moments

Until those are proven, MyKinLegacy is a strong thesis, not yet a validated market.
