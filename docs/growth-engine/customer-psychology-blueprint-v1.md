# Customer Psychology Blueprint v1.0

Status: Growth Engine Sprint 1  
Brand: MyKinLegacy  
Purpose: Explain why Western families may pay to create Family Legacy.

This document uses observations from common family behavior and mature commercial categories. It does not claim clinical psychology research or invented data.

## 1. Core Thesis

People pay for Family Legacy when a life moment makes meaning urgent.

They are not buying a graphic.

They are trying to:

- honor someone
- preserve memory
- give a meaningful gift
- define family identity
- create belonging
- pass values forward
- mark a life transition

## 2. Transformation Model


## 3. Gift Psychology

Meaningful gifts are remembered because they communicate attention.

Expensive gifts can impress.

Meaningful gifts recognize.

Gift value increases when the recipient feels:

- seen
- honored
- remembered
- understood
- connected
- proud

Gift failure occurs when the gift feels generic.

MyKinLegacy must make the gift feel impossible to buy for anyone else in exactly the same way.

## 4. Family Identity Psychology

Family identity is created through shared references:

- stories
- values
- sayings
- rituals
- names
- places
- symbols
- memories

People want family identity because it gives them a way to say:

"This is who we are."

Identity becomes stronger when it can be repeated, displayed, gifted, or passed down.

## 5. Meaning Psychology

Meaning is the reason an object is kept.

Without meaning, an artifact is decoration.

With meaning, an artifact becomes memory.

Meaning is created when:

- a symbol connects to a person
- a story connects to a value
- a gift connects to recognition
- a moment connects to future memory

## 6. Legacy Psychology

Legacy is future-facing.

Parents and grandparents do not only preserve what happened. They preserve what they hope descendants will understand.

Legacy asks:

- What should continue?
- What should be remembered?
- What did we stand for?
- What did we build?
- What should children inherit emotionally?

Legacy products succeed when they let people feel that something important will not disappear.

## 7. Western Family Context

In many Western markets, family identity is often fragmented by:

- migration
- blended families
- surname changes
- geographic distance
- aging parents
- digital photo overload
- weakening rituals
- uncertain ancestry

This creates demand for objects that organize meaning.

MyKinLegacy can serve this need if it avoids false authority and stays emotionally precise.

## 8. Purchase Psychology

People are most likely to buy when:

- there is a deadline
- there is a recipient
- there is emotional pressure
- there is a visible artifact
- there is trust
- the price feels lower than the emotional cost of doing nothing

Strongest purchase triggers:

1. Father's gift
2. Parents anniversary
3. Wedding
4. Grandparent memory
5. Memorial
6. First child
7. Family reunion

## 9. Objection Psychology

Likely objections:

- Is this fake?
- Is this official?
- Is AI making this generic?
- Will it feel personal?
- Is it worth the price?
- Will my family understand it?
- Is this too sentimental?
- Is it private?

The answer must always return to:

private, symbolic, interpreted, meaningful, not official, not proof.

## 10. Strategic Conclusion

MyKinLegacy should not sell novelty.

It should sell emotional completion:

"I finally have something that says what this family means."
