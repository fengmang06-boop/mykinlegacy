# MyKinLegacy First 100 Sales Blueprint v1.0

Status: Go-to-market execution blueprint  
Project: G008  
Primary question: If the site launched today with no brand, no backlinks, no traffic, and no users, how would MyKinLegacy get its first 100 real orders?  
Scope: First customer, first 10 customers, first 100 customers, channel allocation, funnel metrics, 90-day experiments, and CEO dashboard.

This document does not discuss code, product development, page design, Docker, Stripe, database, or implementation.

The goal is not traffic.

The goal is 100 real paid orders from customers who understand what they bought.

## 1. Success Definition

First 100 Sales is complete only when MyKinLegacy has:

1. 100 paid orders from real customers.
2. At least 80 completed collections delivered.
3. At least 30 customer responses or reviews about collection quality.
4. At least 20 customers who say the collection felt personal or meaningful.
5. At least 10 customers who shared the collection with family.
6. Clear data on top 3 buying life moments.
7. Clear data on top 3 objections.
8. Clear data on refund or dissatisfaction causes.
9. At least one repeat or second-order family purchase.
10. Enough evidence to decide whether to continue, reposition, or pause.

A sale does not count as strategically successful if:

- the customer expected official heraldry
- the customer misunderstood the product
- the order was driven only by discounting
- the customer did not complete enough input for identity
- the collection did not reach delivery

The real milestone is not 100 transactions.

It is 100 paid learning events.

## 2. Customer Zero

Customer Zero should not be "everyone."

The strongest first customer profile:

| Attribute | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Age | 32-48 |
| Country | United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia |
| Income | Middle to upper-middle income; comfortable spending $50-$100 on a meaningful gift |
| Life stage | Adult child, married or with family responsibilities |
| Immediate need | Needs a meaningful gift for father, mother, parents, or grandparents |
| Emotional state | Loves family but struggles to express it |
| Trigger | Father's Day, anniversary, birthday, retirement, or parent aging |
| Buying reason today | A gift deadline or emotional moment creates urgency |
| Why MyKinLegacy fits | They want meaning, story, and a keepsake, not another object |

### Customer Zero Example

Name: Sarah  
Age: 38  
Country: United States  
Life stage: Married, two children, parents in their late 60s  
Income: Household income $90k-$160k  
Trigger: Father's 70th birthday is in two weeks  
Problem: Her father is hard to buy for and dislikes generic gifts  
Emotional truth: She wants to honor what he built for the family  
Likely search/ask: "meaningful gift for dad who has everything"  
Why she buys today: The birthday is close, siblings are asking what to get, and she wants something that feels personal  

Customer Zero buys because:

"This might finally say what we have never put into words."

## 3. First 10 Customers

The first 10 customers should be acquired manually, not through passive waiting.

### Step-by-Step Plan

#### Step 1: Pick one life moment

Start with:

Meaningful gift for dad / parents who have everything.

Reason:

- high emotional clarity
- high buyer intent
- easier to explain
- less sensitive than memorial
- broader than wedding

#### Step 2: Create a direct outreach list

Target:

- friends of founder
- family acquaintances
- parent groups
- local community groups
- small Facebook groups
- Reddit threads where promotion is allowed or feedback is requested respectfully
- wedding/gift communities for feedback, not spam

Goal:

Find 50 people who have an upcoming parent gift moment.

#### Step 3: Use a research-first offer

Message:

"I'm testing a new private Family Legacy Collection for people who want a meaningful gift for a parent. It turns family values, stories, and symbols into a keepsake collection. I'm looking for 10 early customers at a founder price in exchange for honest feedback."

Do not lead with AI.

Do not lead with crest.

Lead with meaningful parent gift.

#### Step 4: Conduct short buyer interviews

Ask:

- Who is the gift for?
- What occasion is coming?
- Why is this person hard to buy for?
- What should they feel when they receive it?
- What would make this feel personal?

#### Step 5: Sell before scaling

For the first 10, founder involvement is acceptable.

Goal:

- 10 paid customers
- 10 completed interviews
- 10 delivered collections
- 10 follow-up calls or written feedback

#### Step 6: Capture proof

For each customer:

- why they bought
- what almost stopped them
- what they expected
- what felt personal
- what they shared
- whether they would recommend

## 4. First 100 Customers

The first 100 should be channel-diverse but not scattered.

### Channel Strategy

| Channel | Role | Expected orders | Rationale |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- |
| Gift outreach | Direct first sales | 25 | Fastest path to paid learning |
| Facebook family/gift groups | Warm family-context traffic | 15 | Parent, gift, reunion, and local groups |
| Pinterest | Visual discovery | 10 | Wedding, certificate, wall art, gift inspiration |
| Google organic | Intent capture | 15 | Gift, symbolic crest, family legacy queries |
| AI Search | Advisory discovery | 5 | Early but uncertain |
| Reddit | Objection discovery and niche conversions | 5 | Useful for feedback, lower direct selling |
| Wedding micro-channel | Premium intent | 10 | Strong visual and event demand |
| Referral/family sharing | Natural second-order orders | 10 | From first 50 customers |
| Direct/organic brand | Spillover | 5 | People returning or typing brand |

Total: 100 orders

### First 100 Phases

#### Orders 1-10

Manual outreach, research-first, founder-led.

#### Orders 11-30

Convert strongest life moment into repeatable message.

Likely focus:

- father's gift
- parents anniversary
- meaningful gift for parents

#### Orders 31-60

Add visual discovery and social proof.

Likely focus:

- Pinterest
- family group sharing
- small customer stories

#### Orders 61-100

Scale the top two working entry points.

Likely focus:

- Google intent pages if live
- gift/referral loops
- wedding or parent gift channel depending on early data

Reality check:

In the first 100 sales, founder-led sales should not be considered a weakness. They are the fastest way to learn.

If all first 100 orders require heavy manual persuasion, that is a warning.

If the first 20 manual customers create referral or organic pull, that is a positive signal.

## 6. Conversion Funnel

Funnel:

Visitor -> Explorer -> Journey -> Collection -> Sharing -> Referral

| Stage | Definition | Key metric | Target for first 100 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Visitor | Reaches brand or offer | Qualified visitor count | 3,000-6,000 qualified visitors |
| Explorer | Reads enough to understand offer | Offer comprehension rate | 40%+ |
| Journey | Starts family interview or inquiry | Start rate | 8-15% |
| Collection | Purchases and completes input | Purchase rate | 1-3% cold, 5-15% warm |
| Sharing | Shares with family or responds emotionally | Share/reaction rate | 20%+ |
| Referral | Another family member becomes buyer or lead | Referral signal | 5-10 second-order leads |

### Funnel Failure Signals

| Failure | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Visitors do not explore | Positioning unclear or wrong traffic |
| Explorers do not start | Offer not compelling or trust gap |
| Journey starts but no purchase | Price, expectation, or product uncertainty |
| Purchase but poor feedback | Collection value not strong enough |
| No sharing | Emotional value too weak |
| No referral | Family network loop not working |

## 8. Things We Will NOT Do

We will not:

- burn money on broad ads before offer validation
- buy junk backlinks
- publish low-quality AI articles
- mass-produce surname pages
- chase trends unrelated to family meaning
- pretend to be official heraldry
- use fake scarcity
- discount so heavily that pricing data becomes useless
- spam Reddit or Facebook groups
- force referrals
- hide AI involvement
- make AI the hero
- sell "files" as the value
- build advanced products before first 100 orders
- optimize for traffic before purchase intent
- confuse early learning with scale

The first 100 sales should be earned through clarity, trust, and direct learning.

## 10. Founder Review

If MyKinLegacy does not reach 100 sales in one year, the biggest reason will probably not be technology.

It will be one of these:

1. The offer was not clear enough.
2. The first use case was too broad.
3. The brand sounded like an AI generator instead of a meaningful family gift.
4. Customers did not understand what they would receive.
5. The $69 price was not supported by perceived value.
6. The collection did not feel personal enough.
7. The interview was too long or too shallow.
8. The team waited for organic traffic instead of selling manually.
9. The product did not create enough family sharing.
10. The company kept expanding strategy instead of validating demand.

The founder truth:

The first 100 sales will not come from a perfect brand system.

They will come from finding one painful, emotional, urgent life moment and serving it better than anyone else.

The likely first winning wedge:

"A meaningful family gift for a father or parents who are hard to buy for."

If MyKinLegacy can win that moment, it can earn the right to expand.
