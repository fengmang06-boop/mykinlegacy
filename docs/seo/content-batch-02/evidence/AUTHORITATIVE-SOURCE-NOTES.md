# Content Batch 02 Authoritative Source Notes

Research date: 2026-07-15

These notes support factual boundaries in Content Batch 02. They are not publication copy and do not reproduce long source passages.

## Heraldry

- College of Arms, “FAQs: heraldry”  
  https://www.college-of-arms.gov.uk/resources/faqs  
  Used for: arms do not belong to everyone sharing a surname; a crest is one component of a full heraldic achievement; formal grants and inherited rights are distinct from modern personal artwork.
- College of Arms, “Resources”  
  https://www.college-of-arms.gov.uk/resources  
  Used for: arms concern specific people and families, not a universal surname lookup.

## Family Archives and Digital Preservation

- U.S. National Archives, “How to Preserve Family Archives”  
  https://www.archives.gov/preservation/family-archives  
  Used for: family papers and photographs benefit from intentional handling and preservation.
- U.S. National Archives, “Digitizing Family Papers and Photographs”  
  https://www.archives.gov/preservation/family-archives/digitizing  
  Used for: digitization supports access and sharing; originals should be retained; basic metadata and multiple backups support long-term care.
- U.S. National Archives, “Displaying Family Papers and Photographs”  
  https://www.archives.gov/preservation/family-archives/displaying  
  Used for: display copies can reduce light exposure to originals; framing materials and light conditions matter.
- Library of Congress, “Personal Digital Archiving”  
  https://www.loc.gov/static/programs/digital-preservation/personal-digital-archiving/  
  Used for: personal digital collections require active organization, description, format awareness, and preservation planning.

## Anniversary Conventions

- Time, “Why Are There Special Gifts for Each Anniversary Year?”  
  https://time.com/4771179/history-anniversary-gifts-paper-silver-gold/  
  Used for: silver and gold anniversary customs have documented historical development; broad year-by-year lists developed later.
- Timeanddate.com, “Anniversary and Birthday Traditions Around the World”  
  https://www.timeanddate.com/date/anniversary-gift-traditions.html  
  Used for: anniversary customs and material lists vary across cultures and between the United States and United Kingdom.

## Editorial Use Rules

1. Cite a source only for the narrow claim it supports.
2. Treat anniversary materials as optional conventions, not obligations.
3. Do not generalize one jurisdiction’s heraldic rules to every country.
4. Do not present MyKinLegacy artwork as inherited arms, legal heraldry, genealogy, or proof of status.
5. Do not claim that a digital collection is permanent; recommend active preservation instead.
