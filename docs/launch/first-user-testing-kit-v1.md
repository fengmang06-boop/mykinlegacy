# First User Testing Kit v1.0

Project: Launch Sprint F  
Brand: MyKinLegacy  
Purpose: Give Founder a repeatable first-user testing kit for real market validation.

This kit is for testing whether real people understand, value, and would pay for the current MyKinLegacy offer. It is not a product review, design review, or feature wishlist.

## 1. Founder Test Checklist

Founder must complete the full flow once before inviting users.

Rule: do not explain the product to yourself. Act like a cold visitor.

### Page 1: Home Page

Record:

- First feeling:
- What do I think this sells within 5 seconds?
- Do I understand who this is for?
- Do I feel a reason to continue?
- Do I feel any confusion?
- Would I click `Begin Their Legacy`?
- Score: /10

Pass signal:

Founder can explain the offer in one sentence without using internal language.

Fail signal:

Founder needs the strategy documents to understand the page.

### Page 2: Family Legacy Collection Page

Record:

- First feeling:
- Do I understand what the Collection is?
- Do I understand why it is for parents who already have everything?
- Do I feel the value is emotional, not just digital?
- Do I trust the private/symbolic boundaries?
- Would I continue to create?
- Score: /10

Pass signal:

The page makes the Collection feel gift-worthy.

Fail signal:

It feels like a list of digital artifacts.

### Page 3: Create / Interview

Record:

- First feeling:
- Does the first question feel gift-first?
- Do the questions feel easy to answer?
- Do the questions make me think about a real person?
- Do I feel emotionally closer to the gift?
- Do I feel the interview is too long?
- Score: /10

Pass signal:

Founder can imagine a real parent, grandparent, or family moment while answering.

Fail signal:

It feels like entering prompt ingredients.

### Page 4: Confirm Identity

Record:

- First feeling:
- Does the summary feel personal enough?
- Do I understand what will be prepared?
- Do I trust the symbolic disclaimer?
- Do I feel ready to enter email?
- Do I feel closer to payment or less interested?
- Score: /10

Pass signal:

The page creates a clear "yes, this is what I want" moment.

Fail signal:

The summary feels generic or placeholder-like.

### Page 5: Checkout

Record:

- First feeling:
- Do I understand the price?
- Do I feel safe paying?
- Do the consent statements feel clear?
- Do I understand digital delivery?
- Would I pay with a real card?
- Score: /10

Pass signal:

Founder would proceed without needing to reread the offer.

Fail signal:

Founder hesitates because value or delivery is unclear.

### Page 6: Success / Collection / Vault

Record:

- First feeling:
- Do I understand that the Collection is ready or being prepared?
- Do I know where to receive it?
- Does it feel like a meaningful keepsake?
- Would I send it to family?
- Would I be disappointed after paying?
- Score: /10

Pass signal:

The post-payment moment feels like receiving a Collection, not completing a transaction.

Fail signal:

It feels like a receipt or file access page.

## 2. Cold User Test Script

Use this exact script for each tester.

### Before the Test

"I'm testing an early version of MyKinLegacy. Please use it like you found it online. I am not testing you. I am testing whether the site is clear enough. Please say out loud what you think, especially when something feels confusing, interesting, or not worth paying for."

### Rules for Founder

- Do not explain the product before the test.
- Do not defend the site.
- Do not correct the user unless they are blocked.
- Do not ask leading questions during the flow.
- Do not mention the intended market until after the test.
- Let silence happen.
- Record exact words.

### Task Given to User

"Imagine you need a meaningful gift for parents or grandparents who already have everything. Start on this page and decide whether you would continue."

### During the Test

Ask only neutral prompts:

- "What are you thinking?"
- "What do you think this is?"
- "What would you do next?"
- "What feels unclear?"
- "Would you continue?"

### Payment Moment Prompt

When the user reaches checkout:

"If this were a real checkout for $69, what would you do right now?"

Do not persuade.

### After the Test

Ask the 10 post-test questions below.

## 4. Post-Test Questions

Ask exactly these 10 questions. Do not add more during the first testing batch.

1. In one sentence, what do you think MyKinLegacy sells?
2. Who do you think this is for?
3. What part felt most meaningful?
4. What part felt least convincing?
5. Did this feel like a gift you could give to parents or grandparents?
6. If this cost $69, would you pay today? Why or why not?
7. What made you hesitate before checkout?
8. Did anything feel too much like an AI tool or digital file product?
9. Would you share the finished Collection with family? Who?
10. What one change would make you more likely to buy?

## 5. Reality Score

This is a market score, not a product quality score.

Score each tester from 0 to 100.

| Dimension | Points | What it measures |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Offer Clarity | 15 | User understands what is being sold within 30 seconds. |
| Recipient Fit | 10 | User connects it to parents, grandparents, or family gifting. |
| Emotional Pull | 15 | User feels meaning, recognition, memory, or gratitude. |
| Value Belief | 15 | User believes the Collection could be worth $69. |
| Checkout Intent | 20 | User would seriously consider paying with a real card. |
| Trust | 10 | User feels privacy, delivery, and symbolic boundaries are clear. |
| Share Intent | 10 | User would share with family. |
| Differentiation | 5 | User sees it as more than Etsy, AI image output, or ordinary gifts. |

### Score Bands

| Score | Meaning | Decision |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 80-100 | Strong market signal | Continue testing with more users |
| 65-79 | Promising but unclear | Modify offer, then retest |
| 50-64 | Weak but recoverable | Fix clarity/value before more tests |
| 0-49 | Market not convinced | Stop and rethink Collection or target |

## 6. Decision Rules

### Continue

Continue to a larger user test if:

- Average Reality Score is 75+
- At least 5 of 10 users would seriously consider paying $69
- At least 7 of 10 understand the offer
- At least 5 of 10 say it is gift-worthy
- At least 3 of 10 would share with family
- No critical checkout or delivery confusion appears repeatedly

Next action:

Invite 20 more warm/cold mixed testers and preserve the current positioning.

### Modify

Modify the offer or flow if:

- Average Reality Score is 50-74
- Users like the idea but hesitate at $69
- Users understand the product only after explanation
- Users call it "interesting" but not urgent
- Users compare it to a cheap digital file or generic AI output
- Users want a clearer example before checkout

Next action:

Fix only the highest repeated blocker, then test 5 more users.

Do not add features.

### Stop

Stop this positioning if:

- Average Reality Score is below 50
- Fewer than 3 of 10 users understand the offer
- Fewer than 2 of 10 would consider paying $69
- Most users describe it as just an AI image or digital file product
- Users do not connect it to parents, grandparents, or family gifting
- Users would not share it with family

Next action:

Do not buy traffic. Do not expand channels. Rework the Collection or choose a different buying trigger.

## Founder Testing Rule

The first batch should be small and honest.

Recommended first batch:

- 5 warm users who will be honest
- 5 colder users who do not know the product

Founder should watch behavior more than compliments.

The strongest signal is not:

"This is cool."

The strongest signal is:

"I know exactly who I would give this to."
