# MyKinLegacy Brand Language System v1.0

Status: Strategic language standard  
Primary brand: MyKinLegacy  
Purpose: Establish unified wording across website, product, email, support, SEO, and future customer touchpoints.

This document defines how MyKinLegacy speaks. It protects trust, strengthens emotional clarity, and prevents the brand from sounding like a generic AI tool, fake heraldry service, template marketplace, or legal certification provider.

## 1. Language North Star

MyKinLegacy language should make customers feel:

- Their family story matters.
- They can create something meaningful without perfect historical records.
- The product is personal, private, and complete.
- The brand is honest about symbolic, heritage-inspired output.
- AI is used responsibly as a creative assistant, not as historical authority.

Core sentence:

Create a private family legacy collection shaped by your name, heritage, values, symbols, and story.

## 2. Forbidden Words and Phrases

These words and phrases should not be used in customer-facing promise language.

| Avoid | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| Official coat of arms | Implies legal or heraldic authority |
| Certified family crest | Implies false certification |
| Authentic ancestral crest | Implies historical proof |
| Prove your ancestry | Genealogy proof claim |
| Royal bloodline | Status fabrication risk |
| Noble lineage | Status fabrication risk |
| Legally granted arms | Legal authority claim |
| Heraldic rights | Legal/heraldic authority claim |
| Guaranteed historical accuracy | Unsupported certainty |
| Your real family crest | False authenticity implication |
| AI verified ancestry | AI authority risk |
| Instant official crest | Combines speed with false authority |
| Public gallery by default | Violates privacy posture |
| Cheap AI images | Commoditizes the product |
| Files | Too generic and low-value |
| Downloads only | Reduces collection value |
| Template | Implies generic output |
| Logo | Pulls brand toward business/logo design |
| Clipart | Cheapens artifacts |

Forbidden does not mean these words can never appear in disclaimers or comparison pages. It means they must not appear as promises or product claims.

## 5. Emotional Language

Use emotional language that is grounded, not manipulative.

Preferred emotional themes:

- "something worth keeping"
- "a meaningful gift"
- "honor your family story"
- "preserve what matters"
- "shape a private family identity"
- "carry your family values forward"
- "a keepsake for this generation and the next"
- "turn family details into a lasting collection"

Avoid:

- Fear-based urgency
- Shame about incomplete ancestry
- Claims that a family is incomplete without the product
- Overly dramatic grief language
- Fake ancient destiny language

Emotional language should invite reflection, not pressure.

## 6. Trust Language

Trust language must be visible, plain, and consistent.

Required trust phrases:

- "personalized, AI-assisted, heritage-inspired symbolic design"
- "not an official, legally granted, or historically certified coat of arms"
- "not genealogical proof"
- "private by default"
- "created from your inputs"
- "protected symbols and official emblems cannot be copied"

Supportive trust phrases:

- "We focus on meaning, not false authority."
- "Your collection is symbolic and personal."
- "Heritage language is interpretive, not a verified ancestry record."
- "Your family inputs are used to prepare your private collection."

## 7. CTA Rules

CTAs should feel like beginning, completing, or receiving a meaningful collection.

Primary CTAs:

- Begin Your Collection
- Create My Collection
- Start the Guided Interview
- Shape Your Family Legacy
- Receive Your Collection

Secondary CTAs:

- Explore the Collection
- See What Is Included
- Learn How It Works
- Review the Disclaimer
- Find a Meaningful Gift

Avoid:

- Generate Now
- Download Files
- Buy Now
- Get AI Images
- Make Logo
- Claim Your Official Crest

CTA rules:

- Use creation language before payment.
- Use receiving language after completion.
- Use learning language for trust pages.
- Do not use false urgency by default.

## 8. Collection Language

The product should always feel like a complete collection.

Preferred:

- Family Legacy Collection
- collection artifacts
- crest variants
- transparent crest artifact
- symbol explanation
- family story
- heritage certificate
- collection archive
- private Collection Vault

Avoid reducing the product to:

- files
- images
- generated pictures
- PDFs only
- downloads

Example:

Use: "Your Family Legacy Collection includes crest artifacts, symbolic explanations, a family story, a heritage certificate, and a private Collection Vault."

Avoid: "You get three images and PDF files."

## 9. Identity Language

Identity language must be inclusive and careful.

Preferred:

- "family can be inherited, chosen, blended, rebuilt, or remembered"
- "your family values"
- "your household identity"
- "your shared story"
- "your family roots, as you understand and express them"
- "a symbolic identity shaped by your inputs"

Avoid:

- bloodline-only framing
- purity language
- status hierarchy
- assumptions about surname ownership
- assumptions about one culture, one origin, or one family form

## 10. Storytelling Rules

Storytelling should combine warmth with factual humility.

Rules:

1. Tell the story from the customer's inputs.
2. Use symbolic interpretation, not factual invention.
3. Separate known customer details from creative language.
4. Avoid invented ancestors, dates, places, titles, or verified history.
5. Respect grief and family complexity.
6. Make the story giftable and readable.
7. Keep disclaimers visible where needed.

Storytelling formula:

Inputs -> Values -> Symbols -> Meaning -> Artifact -> Keepsake.

## 11. AI Wording Rules

AI should be framed as assistance, not authority.

Preferred:

- AI-assisted
- AI-generated artwork
- AI-assisted creative interpretation
- guided by your inputs
- reviewed by safety rules
- rendered as a private collection

Avoid:

- AI proves
- AI certifies
- AI discovers your true history
- AI knows your ancestry
- AI official heraldry
- magic

Standard AI sentence:

"MyKinLegacy uses AI-assisted creative tools to prepare personalized, heritage-inspired symbolic artifacts from your family inputs."

## 12. Legal Wording Rules

Legal wording should be clear and calm.

Required disclaimers:

- "MyKinLegacy does not create official, legally granted, or historically certified coats of arms."
- "Collections are personalized, AI-assisted, heritage-inspired symbolic designs."
- "Outputs are not genealogical proof."
- "Digital products may have limited refund eligibility after successful generation and delivery."

Avoid:

- legal jargon when plain language works
- hiding disclaimers in footnotes only
- making certification claims and then disclaiming them later
- vague phrases like "historically accurate" without source boundaries

## 13. SEO Wording Rules

SEO wording must not weaken brand trust.

Rules:

- Use search phrases only when the page clearly explains symbolic limits.
- Pages may mention "family crest generator" but must not promise official crests.
- Prefer "symbolic family crest" when making claims.
- Include FAQ language that distinguishes official heraldry from symbolic design.
- Do not create mass thin surname copy.
- Do not write fake certainty for ranking.

Safe SEO phrasing:

- "family crest-inspired"
- "symbolic family crest"
- "heritage-inspired family gift"
- "personalized family legacy collection"
- "AI-assisted family keepsake"

## 16. Email Tone

Email should feel calm, helpful, and personal.

Tone:

- Warm
- Clear
- Progress-oriented
- Respectful of privacy
- Not overly promotional

Email subject patterns:

- "Your collection is being prepared"
- "Your Family Legacy Collection is ready"
- "A few ideas for your family motto"
- "Preserve this story in your Collection Vault"
- "A meaningful gift for Father's Day"

Avoid:

- hype-heavy subject lines
- fake urgency
- vague "your files are ready" language
- excessive AI emphasis

## 17. Support Tone

Support should be practical, kind, and accountable.

Support principles:

- Acknowledge the customer's purpose.
- Explain status clearly.
- Never blame the customer for unclear inputs.
- Use plain next steps.
- Preserve trust boundaries around refunds, delivery, and disclaimers.
- Handle memorial and family conflict contexts gently.

Preferred support phrases:

- "I can help you locate your Collection Vault."
- "Your collection artifacts are still being prepared."
- "We can review whether regeneration is appropriate."
- "Because this is a personalized digital product, refund eligibility depends on generation and delivery status."
- "We cannot create official arms, but we can help shape a symbolic alternative."

## 20. Strategic Conclusion

The MyKinLegacy language system should make the brand feel warm, trustworthy, emotionally useful, and structurally complete.

The language must continually remind the business:

We do not sell files. We help families receive meaningful artifacts.

We do not sell official status. We help families express symbolic identity.

We do not sell AI novelty. We help families preserve something worth keeping.
