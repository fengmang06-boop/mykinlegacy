## 2. Execution Order

Execution should be ordered by ROI, speed of learning, and risk reduction.

| Rank | Execution item | Why now | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Validate one clear wedge: meaningful gift for father/parents | Highest emotional clarity and fastest path to paid learning | Founder |
| 2 | Run founder-led customer conversations | Fastest way to learn objections and buying language | Founder |
| 3 | Sell first 10 manually | Paid truth beats theoretical strategy | Founder |
| 4 | Capture post-delivery feedback | Determines if collection value is real | Founder + Codex support |
| 5 | Measure interview completion and recognition | Validates whether identity input works | Codex + Founder |
| 6 | Test price points: $39 / $69 / $99 | Confirms willingness to pay | Founder |
| 7 | Create controlled examples and proof language | Reduces buyer uncertainty | Codex with Founder approval |
| 8 | Build minimum traffic capture around top wedge | Focuses traffic on one buying moment | Codex |
| 9 | Start Pinterest and family gift experiments | Tests visual discovery cheaply | Founder / assistant support |
| 10 | Start Google and AI Search foundation | Long-term compounding, slower ROI | Codex + CSO |
| 11 | Test wedding as second wedge | Higher premium potential, but more competitive | Founder |
| 12 | Pause advanced product expansion | Prevents dilution before proof | CEO rule |

Do not follow document creation order.

Follow evidence order:

Conversation -> Paid order -> Delivery -> Recognition -> Sharing -> Repeat.

## 3. First 90 Days

### Week 1

- Select first wedge: meaningful gift for father/parents.
- Define one simple offer in plain language.
- Build outreach list of 50 potential buyers.
- Prepare founder interview script using Golden Questions.
- Define paid test price options.

### Week 2

- Conduct 15 customer conversations.
- Ask only about real upcoming gift moments.
- Record objections and exact language.
- Try to close first 3 paid orders manually.
- Do not optimize site before learning from conversations.

### Week 3

- Deliver first test collections with high founder attention.
- Collect feedback within 24-72 hours.
- Score Family Recognition, Gift Worthiness, and This Feels Like Us.
- Identify first product expectation mismatches.

### Week 4

- Reach 5-10 paid orders.
- Summarize why each buyer purchased.
- Summarize why non-buyers declined.
- Decide whether $69 is viable or needs test adjustment.
- Select top two acquisition messages.

### Week 5

- Create or refine minimum proof assets from real learning.
- Prepare 3 clear example narratives with privacy-safe fictionalization if needed.
- Start Pinterest test batch focused on parent gifts and heritage gifts.
- Start Facebook/community feedback posts where allowed.

### Week 6

- Reach 15-20 paid orders.
- Run interview completion review.
- Identify which questions produce best recognition.
- Remove or deprioritize weak questions from manual interview script.
- Start small wedding wedge conversations.

### Week 7

- Compare father/parents gift vs wedding intent.
- Test AI-assisted wording vs interpretation-first wording in conversations.
- Capture first sharing behavior: who saw the collection, who reacted, who asked.

### Week 8

- Reach 25-30 paid orders.
- Prepare first weekly CEO dashboard.
- Decide top channel for next 30 days based on real orders, not traffic.
- Document top 10 objections and responses.

### Week 9

- Start Google/AI Search foundation around proven wedge only.
- Do not expand to 20 themes.
- Continue founder-led sales until repeatable conversion language exists.

### Week 10

- Reach 40-50 paid orders.
- Analyze refund/disappointment risk.
- Evaluate whether collection opening experience works.
- Test certificate/story/symbol guide perceived value.

### Week 11

- Push second batch of traffic experiments.
- Ask first 20 customers for private sharing behavior.
- Track whether any second-order buyers appear.

### Week 12

- Reach 60-75 paid orders if wedge is working.
- If not, diagnose wedge, offer, price, or trust issue.
- Decide whether to double down on parent gift, shift to wedding, or pause.

### Week 13

- Target 100 orders or clear go/no-go evidence.
- Complete 90-day validation report.
- Decide next phase:
  - continue same wedge
  - add wedding wedge
  - revise pricing
  - revise collection promise
  - pause if kill criteria are met

## 4. Codex Tasks

Codex should do tasks that create execution leverage without replacing founder learning.

### Must Be Codex

- Organize docs and research notes.
- Create structured scripts from approved strategy.
- Convert founder notes into clean experiment records.
- Prepare validation dashboards and templates.
- Draft customer interview scripts.
- Draft privacy-safe example collection copy.
- Audit whether existing site language matches brand system.
- Build or modify implementation only when CEO explicitly reopens development.
- Run local checks when implementation resumes.

### Should Not Be Codex

- Pretend to validate market without real customers.
- Invent customer feedback.
- Decide pricing without evidence.
- Replace founder sales conversations.
- Expand product roadmap before validation.
- Start new strategy loops without CEO request.

## 5. Founder Tasks

The CEO/Founder must personally complete the highest-truth work.

Founder-owned tasks:

1. Talk to real potential buyers.
2. Ask for money.
3. Hear objections directly.
4. Watch where people get confused.
5. Follow up after delivery.
6. Ask whether the recipient reacted.
7. Ask whether they would share it.
8. Ask whether they would buy again.
9. Decide whether to continue, pivot, or kill a wedge.
10. Protect focus from more planning.

Founder weekly minimum:

- 10 direct customer conversations
- 3 sales attempts
- 3 non-buyer interviews
- 1 written learning summary

## 6. CSO Tasks

CSO should be used for strategic synthesis after evidence appears.

Wait for CSO on:

- interpreting first 10 customer interviews
- deciding wedge priority after real data
- refining offer language after objections
- evaluating price test results
- choosing whether to expand to wedding or memory
- reviewing 90-day validation report
- deciding next strategic phase after 100 orders

Do not ask CSO for:

- more long-range product ideas
- more abstract frameworks
- more speculative channels
- new brand directions before validation

## 7. Validation Gates

Do not advance stages without gates.

| Gate | Requirement | Unlocks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gate 1: 10 conversations | 10 real target-buyer conversations completed | First paid manual sales push |
| Gate 2: 3 paid orders | 3 real paid orders from target wedge | Continue offer testing |
| Gate 3: 10 orders | 10 paid orders + feedback from at least 5 | Refine offer and examples |
| Gate 4: 30 orders | Clear top life moment and top objections | Add second channel test |
| Gate 5: 100 Google-indexed public assets | Only after winning wedge is clear | Broader organic foundation |
| Gate 6: 1,000 qualified organic visits | Must show intent, not just traffic | SEO scaling decision |
| Gate 7: 100 orders | 100 real orders with feedback and share data | Next product/channel expansion |
| Gate 8: 1,000 orders | Repeatable sales engine | Team/process scaling |
| Gate 9: Repeat purchase signal | At least 5% customers return or family member buys | Lifetime journey investment |
| Gate 10: Share loop signal | 10%+ customers share or create second-order leads | Referral/sharing system investment |

Gate rule:

No advanced expansion before 100 validated orders.
