# MyKinLegacy Customer Lifetime Journey v1.0

Status: Customer lifecycle strategy  
Project: G006  
Primary question: How does a family move from completely unknown to loyal, advocate, and multi-generation customer?  
Scope: Customer lifetime, trust, emotion, repeat purchase, community, and brand metrics.

This document does not discuss code, database, API, SEO, Prompt, deployment, tools, page creation, or product development.

MyKinLegacy should not think only in conversion funnels.

It should think in family lifetimes.

## 1. Customer Life Stages

| Stage | Customer state | Core question | Brand job | Desired movement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Stranger | Has never heard of MyKinLegacy | "What is this?" | Become understandable and trustworthy | Stranger -> Curious |
| Curious | Notices the idea through search, AI, Pinterest, or family share | "Is this meaningful or gimmicky?" | Explain symbolic, private, family-centered value | Curious -> Explorer |
| Explorer | Reads, compares, tries a free idea, or considers a gift | "Could this work for my family?" | Help them imagine their family inside the collection | Explorer -> Creator |
| Creator | Starts reflecting or answering interview questions | "Can I explain my family?" | Guide reflection without pressure | Creator -> Buyer |
| Buyer | Pays for a collection | "Will this be worth it?" | Reassure through trust, clarity, and expectation-setting | Buyer -> Collector |
| Collector | Receives the Family Legacy Collection | "Does this feel like us?" | Deliver recognition and keepsake value | Collector -> Sharer |
| Sharer | Shows or gifts the collection | "Who else should see this?" | Make sharing dignified, safe, and meaningful | Sharer -> Family Advocate |
| Returning Customer | Comes back for a new life moment | "What else can we preserve?" | Connect future moments to the family archive | Returning Customer -> Legacy Family |
| Family Advocate | Recommends MyKinLegacy to relatives | "This helped our family." | Support organic trust through family proof | Advocate -> New family networks |
| Legacy Family | Uses MyKinLegacy across years or generations | "This is part of how we preserve ourselves." | Become a long-term family identity platform | Multi-generation relationship |

The goal is not to move a user through a funnel once.

The goal is to become the trusted brand a family returns to when life changes.

| Entry point | Typical intent | First trust requirement | Best first promise |

## 3. Trust Journey

Trust is built in layers.

### Trust Building Timeline

| Stage | User concern | What builds trust |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First awareness | "Is this real or just another AI tool?" | Clear positioning: private symbolic family collections |
| First explanation | "Is this fake heraldry?" | Visible disclaimer: not official, legally granted, or historically certified |
| Exploration | "Will this understand my family?" | Examples, questions, values-first language |
| Interview | "Is it safe to share family details?" | Private-by-default language and respectful questions |
| Pre-purchase | "Will this be worth paying for?" | Clear collection promise and emotional use cases |
| Waiting | "Will it actually arrive?" | Calm expectation setting and collection progress language |
| First opening | "Does this feel personal?" | Recognition, story, and symbol explanation |
| Sharing | "Can I show this safely?" | Shareable previews and privacy boundaries |
| Return | "Can this grow with my family?" | Vault, continuity, and future life moments |

Trust grows when MyKinLegacy consistently says what it is and what it is not.

The strongest trust statement:

"We help you create a private, heritage-inspired symbolic collection. We do not claim official heraldry or genealogical proof."

Curiosity -> Reflection -> Emotion -> Pride -> Sharing -> Belonging

### Curiosity

"Could this be something for my family?"

- skepticism
- gift pressure
- identity curiosity

### Reflection

The customer starts answering deeper questions.

- surprise

- vulnerability

### Emotion

Emotion:

- love
- grief
- gratitude
- hope
- responsibility

### Pride

Emotion:

- recognition
- ownership
- confidence
- display desire

### Sharing

Emotion:

- excitement

### Belonging

## 5. Lifetime Product Journey

Product purchase should follow life moments.

| Life moment | Likely purchase | Why now |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First discovery of family identity need | Family Legacy Collection | Customer wants a complete identity artifact |
| Engagement or wedding | Wedding Collection | Two families are becoming one household |
| Father's Day or father milestone | Father Collection | Gift needs gratitude and recognition |
| Mother's Day or mother milestone | Mother Collection | Gift needs warmth and family memory |
| Grandparent aging | Memory Collection | Stories may disappear |
| Parent passed away | Memorial Collection | Family needs remembrance |
| First child | Children Collection | Parents want to pass down values |
| Child asks about roots | Children Collection or Family Story | Family needs child-friendly identity |
| Family reunion | Reunion Collection | Extended family needs shared symbols |
| First home | Wall Art or House Collection | Family identity becomes visible at home |
| Anniversary | Anniversary Collection | Shared years need recognition |
| Family archive begins | Family Vault | Family wants continuity and storage |
| Multi-year relationship | Legacy Updates | Family story evolves |

The product journey should not push everything at once.

It should appear at the right moment.

## 6. Repeat Purchase System

People return when life changes again.

### Why They Return

- a new child is born
- a parent retires
- a grandparent's story needs preserving
- a wedding happens
- a memorial moment arrives
- a family reunion is planned
- a child asks about roots
- an anniversary approaches
- they want to update or expand the family archive

### 1-Year Lifecycle

Likely behavior:

- first collection
- gift sharing
- holiday or anniversary return
- add-on artifact or wall art

Return purchase:

- Father's Legacy Gift
- Anniversary Legacy Gift
- Legacy Wall Art

### 3-Year Lifecycle

Likely behavior:

- new family moments
- wedding, child, home, reunion
- deeper family story interest

Return purchase:

- Wedding Collection
- Children Collection
- Family Reunion Kit
- Family Story Book

### 5-Year Lifecycle

Likely behavior:

- archive mentality begins
- more family members involved
- parent/grandparent memory becomes urgent

Return purchase:

- Memory Collection
- Grandparent Legacy Book
- Family Vault
- Legacy Updates

### 10-Year Lifecycle

Likely behavior:

- next generation asks questions
- old collections gain meaning
- family wants continuity

Return purchase:

- Family Vault
- Children Collection
- Timeline
- Legacy Update
- Multi-generation archive

Repeat purchase is not caused by discounts.

It is caused by recurring family meaning.

## 7. Community Layer

MyKinLegacy community should grow from family networks, not generic social networks.

### Family

The first community is the immediate family:

- spouse
- parents
- children
- siblings
- grandparents

These people validate whether the collection feels true.

### Children

Children create future continuity.

They ask:

- Where did we come from?
- What does our family stand for?
- Who were my grandparents?
- Why does this symbol matter?

### Grandchildren

Grandchildren turn collections into inheritance.

They may discover the collection years later and ask for stories behind it.

### Family Reunion

Reunions create group identity.

They are powerful because one artifact can touch many branches of a family.

### Family Tree

The family tree is context, not the whole product.

MyKinLegacy should use family tree energy to support memory and identity, without claiming genealogical proof.

### Future Community

Future community may include:

- private family vaults
- shared family story spaces
- reunion collections
- legacy updates
- multi-generation artifacts
- family member invitations

The future community should remain private-first.

## 8. Brand Flywheel

The MyKinLegacy flywheel:

Human Need -> Discovery -> Collection -> Sharing -> Trust -> Brand -> Organic Growth -> New Customer

### Human Need

A life moment creates emotional need.

### Discovery

The customer finds MyKinLegacy through search, AI, Pinterest, family, or direct memory.

### Collection

The customer receives a meaningful artifact set.

### Sharing

The collection enters family conversation.

### Trust

Family members see that the brand understands, respects, and preserves meaning.

### Brand

MyKinLegacy becomes associated with family identity moments.

### Organic Growth

More relatives, friends, and connected families become curious.

### New Customer

A new person creates a collection for their own life moment.

The flywheel only works if the collection passes the recognition test.

## 9. North Star Metrics

MyKinLegacy should not optimize only for page views or clicks.

It should optimize for family identity outcomes.

| Metric | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Family Completion Rate | Percentage of customers who complete enough interview depth to form identity |
| Identity Completion Score | How complete the family identity is before collection creation |
| Collection Recognition Score | Likelihood the family says, "This feels like us" |
| Collection Sharing Rate | Percentage of customers who voluntarily share a preview or artifact |
| Gift Reaction Score | Recipient emotional response after receiving collection |
| Family Return Rate | Percentage of families returning for another life moment |
| Legacy Continuation Rate | Percentage of customers who expand, update, or preserve collection over time |
| Multi-Member Engagement | Number of family members involved in creation, review, or sharing |
| Family Conversation Trigger Rate | Percentage of collections that start a family conversation |
| Keepsake Preservation Rate | Percentage of customers who print, save, frame, or revisit collection |
| Trust Clarity Score | Whether users understand symbolic, non-official boundaries |
| Refund Due to Misexpectation Rate | Indicator of broken positioning |
| Referral by Family Context | New customers who arrive through family sharing, not paid incentive |
| Time-to-First-Recognition | How quickly customer sees something personal in the experience |
| Vault Return Frequency | How often customers revisit preserved artifacts |

The highest North Star:

Family Recognition leading to family continuation.

## 10. Founder Review

If MyKinLegacy becomes a Family Identity Platform, customers will not stay for ten years because of a single product.

They will stay because family keeps changing.

A customer may first arrive for a Father's Day gift. Later, they may return for a wedding, a child, a grandparent memory, a family reunion, a memorial, a family archive, and eventually a collection their children can inherit.

They will not leave if MyKinLegacy becomes:

- the place where their family identity is preserved
- the place where life moments become artifacts
- the place where private family memory is safe
- the place that understands symbolic meaning without false claims
- the place their relatives already trust
- the place that grows with the family

The ten-year relationship is not built on product lock-in.

It is built on emotional continuity.

MyKinLegacy wins if a family says:

"When something important happens in our family, this is where we go to preserve it."
