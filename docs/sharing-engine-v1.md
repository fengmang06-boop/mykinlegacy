# MyKinLegacy Sharing Engine v1.0

Status: Word of Mouth strategy  
Project: P003  
Primary question: What makes a family proud enough to share their collection?  
Scope: Human sharing, emotional triggers, family network growth, and long-term word of mouth.

This document does not discuss code, API, database, Stripe, Worker, deployment, page creation, or feature implementation.

The Sharing Engine is not a referral system.

It is the study and design of moments when family meaning naturally wants to move from one person to another.

## 1. Sharing Philosophy

People share when something says something about them, their family, or someone they love.

They do not share because a system asks them to.

They share because:

- they are proud
- they are moved
- they want someone else to feel seen
- they want to honor a person
- they want to start a family conversation
- they want to show identity
- they want to preserve memory
- they want to say, "This is us"

MyKinLegacy sharing must come from meaning, not mechanics.

It is not:

- referral hacking
- discount chasing
- forced virality
- social pressure
- public gallery farming
- "share to unlock"

It is:

- pride becoming visible
- memory becoming shareable
- identity becoming a conversation
- a gift becoming a family story

The core sharing question is:

Who else in the family needs to see this?

## 3. Sharing Moments Timeline

The Collection lifecycle:

Before Purchase -> After Purchase -> First View -> Family Reaction -> Sharing -> Long-term Preservation

### Before Purchase

Sharing behavior:

- buyer asks siblings or spouse for input
- family group discusses gift idea
- buyer collects names, memories, values, or stories

Most shareable moment:

"I found something meaningful we could give Dad."

### After Purchase

Sharing behavior:

- buyer tells co-givers the collection is being prepared
- buyer may ask relatives for memories
- anticipation builds

Most shareable moment:

"Can you send me one memory or phrase that reminds you of Mom?"

### First View

Sharing behavior:

- buyer previews collection
- emotional reaction begins
- buyer decides whether this is giftable

Most shareable moment:

"This actually feels like our family."

### Family Reaction

Sharing behavior:

- recipient reacts
- family members respond emotionally
- first wave of word of mouth begins

Most shareable moment:

"He cried when he opened it."

### Sharing

Sharing behavior:

- collection preview sent to family group
- certificate shown or framed
- story excerpt read aloud
- wedding or reunion asset displayed

Most shareable moment:

"Everyone needs to see this."

### Long-term Preservation

Sharing behavior:

- collection resurfaces during anniversaries, memorials, reunions, holidays
- younger generation asks about it
- family creates additional collections

Most shareable moment:

"We made this years ago, and it still means something."

## 4. Viral Loop Design

The MyKinLegacy loop is not a referral loop.

It is a Family Network loop.

### Organic Family Network Loop

Customer A creates a collection.

Customer A shares it with family.

A family member feels recognition, pride, curiosity, or gift inspiration.

That family member creates a collection for their own household, spouse, parent, child, or event.

The new collection enters another family network.

The cycle repeats.

### Why This Loop Works

Families are naturally networked:

- siblings
- parents
- grandparents
- cousins
- spouses
- in-laws
- children
- wedding guests
- reunion attendees
- family group chats

One collection can touch many emotionally adjacent buyers.

### Loop Conditions

The loop only works if:

- the collection feels personal
- the recipient reacts visibly
- sharing feels dignified
- preview artifacts are safe to share
- the brand is remembered
- the product does not feel cheap or gimmicky

### Loop Failure Conditions

The loop fails if:

- the collection feels generic
- the output looks like an AI novelty
- sharing exposes private details
- the family feels manipulated
- the artifact claims fake authority
- the product is reduced to files

## 5. Organic Growth Channels

| Channel | Natural fit | Best sharing content | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Google | Discovery after hearing about product | Brand search, gift search, symbolic collection search | Weak if brand language unclear |
| Pinterest | Visual inspiration and saving | Certificate previews, wall art, wedding collections, symbol cards | Can become shallow if only visual |
| ChatGPT | Advice and gift recommendations | Meaningful gift, family legacy, preservation questions | Needs clear, trustworthy positioning |
| Gemini | Search-assisted recommendations | Gift and family identity explainers | Needs structured language |
| Claude | Reflective, long-form advice | Family story, legacy letters, values | Needs depth and nuance |
| Reddit | Authentic discussion and skepticism | Honest explanations, user stories, trust boundaries | Direct promotion can backfire |
| Facebook | Family and older audience sharing | Parent gifts, anniversary, memorial, reunion | Privacy sensitivity |
| Instagram | Emotional visual sharing | framed certificate, wedding, gift reaction | Can over-prioritize aesthetics |
| Email | Family coordination and follow-up | gift invite, memory request, collection ready | Spam risk if overused |
| Family Group Chats | Strongest natural channel | preview, certificate, story excerpt, reaction | Must protect private details |

### Best Channels for Human Sharing

1. Family Group Chats
2. Facebook family networks
3. Pinterest for visual inspiration
4. Email for coordinated family gifts
5. ChatGPT/AI search for advisory discovery
6. Instagram for public-safe visual moments

The strongest sharing channel is not social media.

It is the family group chat.

## 6. Collection Sharing Experience

If a user chooses to share, they should share meaning, not raw downloads.

Shareable objects:

| Share object | Purpose | Safe sharing level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Collection Preview | A curated glimpse of the collection | Medium, user-controlled |
| Certificate Preview | Giftable and ceremonial | High if private details are controlled |
| Story Preview | Emotional excerpt | Medium, may contain sensitive details |
| Symbol Guide Preview | Explains meaning without full archive | High |
| Gift Experience Preview | Shows why it was created | Medium |
| Framed Mockup | Shows display value | High |
| Wedding Preview | Useful for guests and planning | High |
| Reunion Preview | Useful for event sharing | High |
| Memorial Preview | Sensitive tribute | Low to medium, careful consent |

### What Should Be Shared

Share:

- a short collection title
- one visual artifact
- one sentence of meaning
- one symbol explanation
- optional gift message
- brand attribution if appropriate

Do not share by default:

- full family story
- private family answers
- sensitive grief details
- full vault access
- private token links
- raw artifact package

### Ideal Share Copy

"We created this as a private family legacy collection to honor Dad and the values he passed down."

"This is the symbolic collection we made for our wedding, built around both families and the home we are creating together."

"We made this for our parents' anniversary. It tells the story of what they built for all of us."

## 7. Emotional Flywheel

The MyKinLegacy emotional flywheel:

Emotion -> Story -> Identity -> Sharing -> Trust -> Brand -> More Sharing

### Emotion

A life moment creates feeling:

- love
- pride
- grief
- gratitude
- belonging
- celebration
- responsibility

### Story

The feeling becomes a story:

- what someone built
- what should be remembered
- what a family stands for
- what a new marriage begins

### Identity

The story becomes identity:

- values
- symbols
- motto
- visual artifacts
- collection narrative

### Sharing

The identity becomes shareable:

- a gift reaction
- a preview
- a certificate
- a family conversation
- a framed artifact

### Trust

Family members see that the product is meaningful and respectful.

### Brand

MyKinLegacy becomes remembered as the place that made the family feel seen.

### More Sharing

Another family member creates a collection for a new moment.

The flywheel works only when meaning is real.

## 8. Things We Never Do

MyKinLegacy must never:

- incentivize fake sharing
- force referral
- spam family members
- require sharing to access the collection
- reduce meaning into discounts
- pressure grief-based sharing
- create public galleries by default
- expose private collection links
- make customers feel used for growth
- turn family emotion into a gimmick
- overbrand private artifacts
- imply that not sharing means the collection failed

Sharing must remain optional, dignified, and human.
